## ABOUT PAGE

### Page heading
About

### Lead paragraph (large, typography-forward)
I'm Mukul Dharpure — a product manager who builds and ships autonomous robots and AI products. I've spent the last five years launching commercial robotics products and delivering them into Fortune 500 factories across India, Thailand, and Singapore.

### The work I do
I'm most useful in the space where **product strategy meets field reality** — where a PRD has to survive a plant floor, where a roadmap has to match customer readiness, and where a single miscalibrated sensor can halt a shift. That's the work I love.

I own products end-to-end: discovery, PRDs, prototyping, launch, deployment, and the long tail of customer success that comes after. I've done it for autonomous forklifts in automotive plants, AMRs in construction and real estate, and a lawnmower that had to mow a lawn in the rain without calling me at 2am.

### What I've shipped
- **2 commercial products launched 0→1** at Flo Mobility — an autonomous material-handling AMR and an autonomous lawnmower, both from market research through commercial release.
- **Scaled a fleet from 0 to 35+ robots** across 10 Indian cities and enterprise sites including L&T, Godrej, Sobha, and K2K — maintained at 95%+ uptime.
- **Delivered enterprise programs** for Ford Motors (Thailand), Caterpillar (Singapore), and Coca-Cola (India) — owning customer relationships, budgets, vendor programs, and shift-to-shift operations.
- **Designed an internal Fleet Management System** from scratch with a 2-engineer team — the tool that kept the entire fleet observable and maintainable.
- **Represented the company internationally** at GITEX Global 2022 (Dubai) and Bangalore Tech Summit 2024.

### How I work
I believe technical credibility is a PM superpower. I can read a ROS log, calibrate a SICK LiDAR, validate a navigation stack, and argue about path-planning edge cases with the engineers building them — and I think that's what makes me a better product manager, not a worse one. It means my PRDs are buildable, my timelines are honest, and my engineering teams trust me when I say "we're shipping this."

I spend most of my time at the intersection of three things: **the customer** (what actually breaks on their floor), **the engineering team** (what's possible this quarter), and **the business** (what we need to charge, ship, and learn). Good product decisions live in that triangle.

## Copy notes for the designer

- **Headline size:** 56–72px desktop, 36–44px mobile. Tight line-height (0.95–1.0).
- **Sub-headline:** 18–22px, 1.5 line-height, max 640px width for readability.
- **Eyebrow tag:** 12–14px uppercase, tracked out (0.1em), muted color.
- **Numbers in impact strip:** monospaced or tabular-nums, large (40–56px), labels tiny (12px uppercase).
- **About page body:** 18–20px, max 680px column width. No walls of text.
- **Use pull quotes** for the two "philosophy" paragraphs in About ("technical credibility is a PM superpower" / "Good product decisions live in that triangle").
- **Tone:** No exclamation marks. No emojis. No "passionate about." Let the numbers do the talking.
