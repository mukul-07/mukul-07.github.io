04 · Decision log

Bets I made and why

Selected · five from a longer log

2021 · Q2Pre-MVP

Said no to 3D LiDAR for v1.

Hardware team argued for a 16-channel 3D LiDAR for robustness in dynamic sites. I cut it. 2D LiDAR + ultrasonics covered our actual obstacle envelope (people, pallets, carts) at one-third the BOM. Saved ~₹2.4 L per unit.

Hindsight · Right call for v1–v2. By v3 we hit edge cases (overhead obstacles, glass walls) where 3D would have helped. Slated for v4.
Win
2022 · Q1Pre-launch

Cut autonomous charging from MVP.

The team wanted auto-dock at v1. I argued construction sites have unpredictable layouts; the dock would relocate weekly. Shipped manual swap-and-charge instead — site supervisor swaps the battery in 90 sec.

Hindsight · Customers didn't miss it. We added it in v3 when sites stabilised. MVP shipped 4 months earlier.
Win
2022 · Q3Post-launch

Pushed back when L&T asked for full payload customisation.

L&T's procurement wanted a per-site bespoke payload module. Said no; offered three standard payload trims (flat, cage, bin). Lost a week in negotiation.

Hindsight · Right call. Bespoke would have killed the platform. Two of the three trims account for 90 % of orders today.
Win
2023 · Q2Mid-cycle

Built the FMS in-house instead of licensing.

Licensing options existed (e.g. Meili, Freedom Robotics). Chose to build. Wanted control over multi-tenant data and customer-facing analytics.

Hindsight · Mixed. Got the analytics right and customers loved it. Cost us ~6 engineer-months I could have spent on nav reliability — which became the #1 ticket source for two quarters.
Mixed
2023 · Q4Scale phase

Under-invested in remote diagnostics.

As the fleet crossed 20 units, we still triaged faults via WhatsApp screenshots from site supervisors. I prioritised features over diagnostics for two quarters.

Hindsight · Wrong call. Cost us 2 customer escalations and one renewal that came in late. Diagnostics shipped Q1 2024 and was the highest-leverage thing we did all year.
Miss

06 · Retrospective

Honest read on the program

Written 2024 · revised 2026

+ Won
Picked the right wedge. Construction-finishing was an under-served niche where 2D nav + heavy payload was a defensible product. Customers paid full price; uptime targets were met.
+ Won
Resisted feature bloat from procurement. Held the platform to three payload trims even when individual deals would have been bigger.
– Missed
Diagnostics. Should have built remote-debug tooling at unit 5, not unit 20. Cost real escalations and one renewal.
– Missed
Underestimated the ops cost of a heterogeneous customer base. Each enterprise required a custom integration ritual we should have systematised by year 2.
→ Next time
Build diagnostics and onboarding-as-product before the 10th unit ships. The single biggest leverage is reducing what your humans have to do per deployment.
