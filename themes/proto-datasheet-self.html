05 · Decision log

Career-level bets I've made

The product-level log lives inside each deployment.

2020 · Q3Career move

Joined Flo Mobility as founding PM instead of going to a tier-1 SaaS company.

Two offers on the table: a senior PM seat at a well-funded SaaS scale-up, and the founding-PM seat at a 6-person robotics startup with no revenue. Took Flo. Bet that hardware + autonomy was a wave that would matter more than another vertical SaaS, and that founding-PM exposure would compound faster than ladder-climbing.

Hindsight · Right call. The five years gave me end-to-end ownership most PMs spend a decade trying to earn.
Win
2022 · Q1Product strategy

Pushed Flo to ship MMR before the lawnmower, against initial founder preference.

The founders favoured the outdoor lawnmower as the wedge — bigger TAM, more glamorous demo. Argued for indoor MMR first: shorter sales cycle, faster iteration loop, customers already willing to pay. Took two months of disagreement to land it.

Hindsight · Right call. MMR became the company's revenue engine; lawnmower followed once the FMS was hardened by MMR's deployments.
Win
2023 · Q2Org design

Built a customer-ops function instead of hiring a third PM.

Fleet hit ~20 units; eng was getting buried in customer escalations. The default move was to hire another PM. Hired two field application engineers (FAEs) instead — closer to customers, took the unstructured load off product.

Hindsight · Mixed. FAEs were the right move, but I should have shipped remote-diagnostics tooling first so they had something to use.
Mixed
2023 · Q4Personal

Under-invested in writing and external presence for two years.

Heads-down on product. Wrote almost nothing public — no blog posts, no LinkedIn, no talks. Optimised for shipping over visibility.

Hindsight · Wrong call. Made the EU/USA job search harder; recruiters can't find what isn't published. Fixing now.
Miss
2026 · Q1Career move

Looking outside India for the next role, with sponsorship needed.

Strong offers exist in India. Choosing to look at EU and USA instead — closer to the next wave of robotics customers (auto, logistics, warehousing) and to the talent density that will define the field for the next decade.

Hindsight · Pending. Will rev this entry in 12 months.
